<span style="font-size:24pt;color:#000ff;"><b>An Introduction to Systems Thinking and Wicked Problem Solving™</b></span>

<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>HOW TO RUN</b></span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>DRAW TOAST WORKSHOPS</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">DrawToast workshops are a great way to get groups to think freshly about mental models. In just 3 minutes, each person sketches a diagram of how to make toast. When comparing diagrams, people are shocked at how diverse the diagrams are, revealing a wide range of models of what's important in making toast. It's a great launch pad for  drawing out what's really important to the group.</span>




<p style="text-align:center;margin:0"><span style="font-size:24pt;color:#000ff;"><b>There are 8 Simple Steps to the DrawToast Exercise:</b></span>

</p>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 1</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Prepare</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">Get the ingredients: felt markers, thick paper stock, sticky notes or index cards, and masking tape. Stage your room with tables, chairs, and a clear wall where you can post your work. It's important to have enough room for all participants to see everyone's creations.</span>

<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 2</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Invite</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">In your invitation, set expectations that your meeting will focus on building a systems model  of an important challenge - clarifying your vision, improving cash flow, figuring out the next bold challenge. Begin with a simple design exercise.</span>


<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 5</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Video</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">Play the</span> <span style="color:#000ff;"><b>TED.com DrawToast video</b></span> <span style="color:#000ff;">and let it explain the big ideas about systems thinking. After it plays, ask the group how many nodes they drew and what kind?</span>

<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 6</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Draw Your Challenge</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">Have people draw a picture how to improve what what they are working on as a group. This can include almost anything, strategic or tactical. See 'Draw Questions' for inspiration. Make sure people draw individually and in silence.</span>



<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 3</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Conduct</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">Run the meeting informally. Hand out markers and paper to everyone and</span> <span style="color:#000ff;"><b>ask people to draw a picture of</b></span> <span style="color:#000ff;"><b><i>how to make toast</i></b></span><span style="color:#000ff;"><b>.</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;"><b>Give them 2-3 minutes.</b></span>
<span style="color:#000ff;">You may want to play toast-making music...</span>

<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 7</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Share</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">Have people work at tables. showing and explaining their diagrams. Compare and contrast the diagrams and see what is similar and different between them. What links and nodes are common?</span>


<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 4</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>Reflect</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">Have each person hold up their drawing for all to see. (Let the laughter start) Have the group place their drawings on a large wall space and comment on the drawings; pointing out which are simple and complex, which have people and which don't.</span>

<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;">Step 8</span>
<span style="font-size:20pt;color:#000ff;"><b>System</b></span>
 
<span style="color:#000ff;">If you have the time, have the group develop a systems diagram of the challenges using sticky notes and drawn links. Building on the previous individual diagrams, have groups of 4-6 people create nodes and links to to solve the challenges.</span>
